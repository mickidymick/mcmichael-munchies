Sourdough Bread

My favorite sourdough bread recipe.

Ingredients

  • 100 g sourdough starter
  • 225 g bread flour
  • 225 g all purpose flour
  • 300 g water
  • 10 g salt

Instructions

  1. Add warm water and the starter to a bowl. Wisk it until the starter has mixed into the water.

  2. Measure out the bread flour, all purpose flour, and salt and add them all to a bowl. Thouroughly mix them together and add them to the water and starter mixture.

  3. Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel and let the dough rest for 30 minutes.

  4. After the 30 minute rest period, give your dough its first set of stretch and folds. Recover the bowl and set asside for another 30 minutes.

  5. After the 30 minute rest period, give your dough its second set of stretch and folds. Recover the bowl and set asside for another 30 minutes.

  6. After the 30 minute rest period, give your dough its third and final set of stretch and folds.

  7. Now that the stretch and folds or finished cover the dough back up and let it rest for the rest of the bulk fermentation process. This should take between 3.5 - 5 more hours.

  8. After the bulk ferment its time to do the preshape. Take out the dough and place it smooth side up.

  9. Sprinkle some flour on the dough and gently shape it into a ball with your hands or a bench scraper. Then cover the dough and let rest for another 30 minutes.

  10. Now shape the dough into the whatever shape banneton you have. I preffer an oval-shaped loaf.

  11. To shape a batard, or oval-shaped loaf, start by sprinkling some flour on the countertop and place top or smooth side of your dough down.

  12. Press the dough down gently and stretch into a rectangle. Flip the right third of the dough over the middle section and then the left third over that section.

  13. Flip the dough long ways pointing away from you and slowly tuck and roll the dough away from you until it is rolled up.

  14. Grab the edges of the spiral on the top side and pull them down covering the spiral. This just makes it look a bit prettier.

  15. Add some flour to your banneton and place your dough in it seam side up. Place in the fridge for at least 6 hours but I generaly do so for about 10 - 12.
